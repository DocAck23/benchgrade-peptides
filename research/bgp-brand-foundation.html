Decision 2

Pick the positioning statement

The two-line read: what we sell + how we conduct ourselves. The second line is what licenses the monthly virtue.
Variant 1 · Made with conscience
Bench Grade Peptides — research compounds, made with conscience.
A small American laboratory supplying bulk synthetic peptides for independent research. Each batch is signed, sealed, and dispatched under a virtue we hold ourselves to that month.
Soft-spoken. "Conscience" reads as ethical lineage rather than vow. Closest to the "atelier laboratory" reading. Lower-pressure.
Pick V1
Variant 2 · Bound by virtue
Bench Grade Peptides — research compounds, bound by virtue.
A small American laboratory supplying bulk synthetic peptides to independent researchers. Every batch ships under the virtue we hold ourselves to that month — stamped in wax, signed by hand, answerable in full.
Harder. "Bound by virtue" + "answerable in full" carries a vow weight. Closest to the founder's-circle reading. Highest emotional pressure.
Pick V2
Variant 3 · Under oath
Bench Grade Peptides — bulk research peptides, dispatched under oath.
A small American laboratory. Twelve virtues a year — one per month, pressed in wax on every box. The month we fall short of the virtue, we say so.
Boldest. "Under oath" + the public accountability clause ("we say so") makes the heartbeat falsifiable. Highest trust signal, also highest commitment — you'd have to actually live it.
Pick V3
Variant 4 · Hybrid · "A virtue per month"
Bench Grade Peptides — research-grade synthetic peptides, a virtue per month.
We're a small American laboratory supplying independent researchers. Every month we choose one virtue and hold our work to it. The virtue is pressed in wax on the box.
Plain language version. The mechanism (one virtue per month, in wax) is foregrounded; the moral language is gentler. Most legible to a first-time reader. The "atelier" register without the "founder's circle" pressure.
Pick V4
